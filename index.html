ABOUT

As Featured on Westword →

Elijah Sylar is a Denver-based painter exploring transformation, consciousness, and the raw complexity of human experience through large-scale abstract and surreal works.

I've been painting since before I could write - it's the language I've spoken longest. At 15, I had my first group show at the Museum of Contemporary Art Houston, launching years of public exhibitions and recognition in the art scene. My work draws deep inspiration from Jackson Pollock's emotional intensity, Salvador Dalí's dreamlike surrealism, and René Magritte's exploration of perception and reality.

After seven years working as a behavioral health therapist with nonverbal autistic children, I learned to communicate through visual language in ways words never could. That experience taught me how powerful images can be - how color, texture, and composition can express what language fails to capture.

My practice evolved through a three-year cross-country journey through 47 states, where I painted my way through profound personal transformation. Each canvas became a meditation on identity, emotion, and what it means to be human in all its messy, complicated glory.

Whether working on intimate portraits or sprawling abstracts, I'm chasing that honest connection between the canvas and whoever stands in front of it - creating spaces where vulnerability, chaos, and beauty collide.
